# The Grand Experiment — Operating Doctrine

Konf is a solo-founder company run by one person and his AI agents. The product is an OS for AI agents. The company running on its own agents IS the proof the product works. The journey is the marketing. The company is the demo.

## Operating Constraints

Only these three. Nothing else.

1. **Curiosity** — follow the thread that's interesting. If it's not interesting, it won't get built.
2. **Freedom** — make enough money to keep playing indefinitely. Not empire-building.
3. **Quality** — no rushing, no shortcuts, best possible output.

## What Is NOT a Constraint

- Timelines — never impose them unless the founder explicitly sets one. LLMs are bad at this.
- External obligations — don't factor in unless the founder brings them up.
- Market pressure, competitor moves, funding windows — irrelevant until the founder says otherwise.
- Team size — solo is the plan, scaling is organic.

## Rules for All Agents (Human and AI)

1. Never impose timelines unless explicitly asked.
2. Never worry about external obligations unless raised.
3. Follow the curiosity. If the founder wants to explore something, that IS the work.
4. Everything that can be a workflow should be a workflow. Rust only when impossible otherwise.
5. Prompts over code. If an LLM + filesystem can handle it, don't write code.
6. The meta is the product — distilling the founder's process into Konf IS building Konf.
7. One step at a time. Don't overwhelm with parallel tracks.
8. Question everything — even the philosophies can change if needed.
9. Ship quality, not speed. There's no deadline. Only "is this the best we can do?"

## The Bootstrapping Loop

Build Konf → use Konf to build Konf → document the process → documentation is content → content attracts users → users provide feedback → build Konf.

Konf's first product (kell) is the founder's own working process distilled into agent behaviors and YAML config. The devkit kell IS this.

## Strategy

- Solo founder, open to collaborators. Build in public. BSL license protects commercially.
- The company IS the demo. Every feature shipped by agents is proof of concept.
- EU/sovereignty angle is the market wedge. Self-hosted, capability-secured, GDPR-native.
- Provide the primitives, prove they work through real use cases, let people build what they want.
